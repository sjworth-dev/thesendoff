## Golf

### TPC Louisiana
*The Big One*

Home of the Zurich Classic. Championship course designed by Pete Dye. If your crew takes golf seriously, this is the play.

- **Location:** Avondale (45 min from French Quarter)
- **Green fees:** $150-250 depending on season
- **Book ahead:** Required, especially weekends

### City Park Golf Course
*The Local Pick*

Recently renovated 18-hole course in beautiful City Park. Solid course, good price, close to the city.

- **Location:** Mid-City (15 min from French Quarter)
- **Green fees:** $50-80
- **Book ahead:** Recommended for groups

### Audubon Golf Course
*The Quick Round*

18 holes in Audubon Park. Not challenging for serious golfers, but convenient and scenic.

- **Location:** Uptown (10 min from French Quarter)
- **Green fees:** $30-50
- **Book ahead:** Walk-ons usually fine

### English Turn Golf & Country Club
*The Upscale Option*

Private club that allows outside play. Jack Nicklaus design. Great condition, full experience.

- **Location:** English Turn (30 min from French Quarter)
- **Green fees:** $100-150
- **Book ahead:** Required

## Fishing

### What to Expect

Most charters include:
- All tackle and bait
- Fishing license
- Cooler with ice for your catch
- Captain who knows the spots

You bring: sunscreen, food/drinks (beer allowed), and a good attitude.

### Half-Day Trips (4-5 hours)
Perfect for bachelor parties. You're back by early afternoon with time to recover.

- **Cost:** $150-200/person (4-6 guys per boat)
- **Book ahead:** 2-3 weeks minimum

### Recommended Charters

**Cajun Fishing Adventures**
Solid reputation, good for groups. They'll split you across multiple boats.

**Louisiana Fishing Charters**
Good for beginners. Patient captains, fun experience.

**Reel NOLA Fishing**
Closer to the city, shorter drive time.

## Casino

### Harrah's New Orleans
*The Only Game in Town*

Louisiana's only land-based casino, right on Canal Street. Open 24 hours.

**What they have:**
- Full poker room
- Table games (blackjack, craps, roulette)
- Slots
- Sports betting

**Bachelor party tips:**
- Thursday-Saturday nights are busiest
- Poker room takes reservations for groups
- Drink service is free if you're gambling
- The steakhouse (Besh Steak) is actually good

## Swamp Tours

Don't write this off. A swamp tour is legitimately one of the most memorable things you can do in Louisiana.

### What You'll Do
- Ride an airboat through the bayou
- See alligators (guaranteed)
- Learn about Cajun culture
- Drink beer on a boat

### Recommended

**Cajun Encounters**
Most popular option. Boats leave from Slidell (30 min from city). They offer hotel pickup.

- **Duration:** 2-3 hours total
- **Cost:** $30-40/person
- **Best time:** Morning (cooler, more active wildlife)

**Airboat Adventures**
Smaller operation, more authentic feel. Better for groups who want less tourist, more real.

## Other Options

### Gun Range

**NOLA Tactical**
Indoor range with rentals. Full auto available (really). Good for a morning activity.

- **Location:** Metairie (20 min from French Quarter)
- **Cost:** $50-100/person depending on rentals

### Axe Throwing

**Stumpknockers**
Exactly what it sounds like. Good for competitive crews.

- **Location:** Warehouse District
- **Cost:** ~$30/person for an hour

### Escape Room

**The Escape Experience**
Multiple rooms with different themes. Good for groups up to 8.

- **Location:** CBD
- **Cost:** ~$35/person

### Food Tour

**Tastebud Tours**
Walking tour through the French Quarter with food stops. More sophisticated option.

- **Duration:** 3 hours
- **Cost:** ~$70/person
